Help
Frequently asked questions

What is TamuFeeds?

TamuFeeds connects restaurants with surplus, still-good food to children's homes and NGOs nearby, so it gets eaten instead of thrown away.

Who can join?

Any restaurant or food business with surplus food to give, and any registered NGO or children's home that can collect it.

Does it cost anything?

No. TamuFeeds is free for both restaurants and NGOs.

How long do NGOs have to collect a claimed listing?

Once claimed, there's a 3-hour pickup window. If it's not marked picked up in time, the listing automatically becomes available again for others.

Is my information safe?

Your account details are stored securely and only shared with the other party once a claim is made, so pickup can be coordinated. See our Privacy Policy for details.

How do I report a problem?

Email us at hello@tamufeeds.com and we'll get back to you.

Legal
Privacy Policy

This is a starter policy for TamuFeeds and should be reviewed by a legal professional before public launch.

What we collect

When you sign up, we collect your email, organization name, location, and phone number. When you post or claim a listing, we store the details of that listing or claim.

How we use it

Your information is used to operate the platform — matching restaurants with NGOs, showing listings on the map and board, and enabling communication needed to coordinate a pickup.

Who can see it

Your organization name and location are visible to other logged-in users on the platform. Your phone number is only shared with the other party once a claim is made.

Where it's stored

Data is stored with Supabase, a hosted database provider, and protected with row-level access rules so accounts can only modify their own data.

Contact

Questions about your data? Email hello@tamufeeds.com.
